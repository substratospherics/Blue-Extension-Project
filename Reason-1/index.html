Why is blue so rare in nature?

REASON 1

Blue pigments, such as anthocyanins and delphinidins, are less frequent in plants and animals than other colors because they are more difficult to produce and are not as important for survival and reproduction.

  • These blue pigments are often produced as a secondary metabolite, which means they are not necessary for basic metabolic processes and may have developed for specific purposes such as enticing pollinators or repelling herbivores. Furthermore, rather than being a constant feature, blue pigments are frequently created in reaction to environmental stresses like UV radiation.
  • In contrast, pigments such as chlorophyll in plants and carotenoids in animals are necessary for photosynthesis and visual communication, respectively, and are therefore more commonly found.
  • To elaborate, chlorophyll, the green pigment found in plants, is required for photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy in the form of glucose. Chlorophyll absorbs light primarily in the blue and red spectrums, reflecting green light and giving plants their distinctive color. Chlorophyll pigments are found in chloroplasts, which are photosynthesis-specialized organelles found in plant cells. Because photosynthesis is necessary for plant survival and growth, chlorophyll can be found in almost all plants.
  • Carotenoids, on the other hand, are pigments found in animals, specifically in their skin, feathers, and scales. They are responsible for many animals' yellow, orange, and red colors. Carotenoids do not participate in photosynthesis, but they do play a role in visual communication in animals, such as attracting mates, defending territory, and camouflaging. Carotenoids are also beneficial to animals because they act as antioxidants, preventing cell damage by neutralizing harmful molecules known as free radicals.
  • As a result, chlorophyll and carotenoids are both important pigments for their respective organisms. Chlorophyll is required for photosynthesis, which is necessary for plant survival and growth, whereas carotenoids are required for visual communication and have antioxidant properties. As a result, they are more common than blue pigments.
